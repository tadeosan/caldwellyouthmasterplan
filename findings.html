Research Findings

Our comprehensive research process included surveys, focus groups, interviews with stakeholders, and analysis of existing youth programs. Below are the key findings that will inform the revised Caldwell Youth Masterplan.

Key Findings by Category

Educational Needs

68% of youth reported needing additional academic support outside of school hours
  • Tutoring programs are in high demand, especially for math and science
  • Access to technology remains a challenge for approximately 22% of youth
  • College preparation resources are utilized by less than 30% of eligible high school students
  • Literacy rates among younger children (ages 6-10) have declined by 8% since 2020

Recommended Focus: Expanding after-school academic programs and improving technology access for home learning.

Mental Health

47% increase in youth seeking mental health services since 2020
  • Anxiety and depression were reported as top concerns among teens
  • Waiting periods for counseling services average 3-4 weeks
  • School counselors report being severely understaffed (1:420 student ratio)
  • Peer support programs show promising results but lack proper funding

Recommended Focus: Expanding mental health staff, reducing wait times, and strengthening peer support networks.

Recreational Activities

52% of youth report that cost is a barrier to participating in extracurricular activities
  • Sports participation has declined by 15% over the past five years
  • Arts and music programs are the most requested additions to current offerings
  • Community centers are operating at reduced hours due to staffing shortages
  • Outdoor recreation spaces are underutilized and often in need of maintenance

Recommended Focus: Scholarship programs for activities, expanded arts offerings, and revitalization of outdoor spaces.

Career Development

73% of teens expressed interest in gaining work experience before graduating high school
  • Internship opportunities are limited, with only 35 placements available annually
  • Technical and vocational training programs are at capacity with waiting lists
  • Entrepreneurship education was identified as a significant gap in current offerings
  • Local businesses expressed willingness to participate in youth employment initiatives

Recommended Focus: Expanding internship programs, developing entrepreneurship education, and creating youth-business partnerships.

Research Methodology

Our findings are based on data collected between January and April 2025, including:

  • 1,200+ survey responses from youth ages 10-18
  • 24 focus groups with participants from diverse backgrounds
  • 42 interviews with community stakeholders
  • Analysis of participation data from existing youth programs
  • Comparative analysis with five similar communities

All research protocols were approved by an independent ethics review board to ensure participant confidentiality and data integrity.

Next Steps

These findings will directly inform the priorities and strategies in our revised masterplan. We invite community feedback on these findings as we move forward with developing specific initiatives to address the identified needs.
